About the Author

Hello — I'm Joey Zarra, an undergraduate student learning about information systems, design, and the ways technology changes how we make decisions. I enjoy exploring small changes that improve everyday life — from making streets friendlier to choosing tools that reduce friction.

My interests include:

  • Geographic Information Systems and spatial thinking
  • Design for public spaces
  • Human-centered tools and productivity

When I'm not digging into design or data, I like to test small experiments: try new note-taking workflows, build tiny websites, and take long walks to observe how people use shared spaces. This blog is a place to record those experiments and share short reflections that might be useful to classmates and community members.
